Lens Power
Focus-Shift Scanner

No reference image or printed pattern needed. Place glasses in front of your camera — the app measures how much the camera has to refocus to stay sharp, and calculates lens power from that shift. 10 readings, weighted median, fully automated.

🎯
Focus-Shift Method
Camera locks focus on distant object, then re-focuses through lens. Power = 1/d_lens − 1/d_ref
🔒
Focus Lock + Unlock
Locks focus for calibration, unlocks for lens measurement. No manual settings needed.
🔁
10-Cycle Averaging
Repeats 10 times and uses weighted median with IQR outlier rejection for accuracy
📐
No Pattern Required
Just point at any distant real-world object. No checkerboard, no printed grid needed.
🌀
Cylinder Detection
Uses H/V Sobel sharpness asymmetry to estimate cylinder power and axis
📊
Live Focus Graph
Real-time sharpness and focus distance plotted for each cycle
📋 What you need:
Spectacle glasses to test Phone with back camera A distant object to focus on (wall, window frame, etc.) Good lighting
⚠ Best results require a camera that exposes the focusDistance API (most Android/Chrome devices). iPhones may use sharpness-comparison fallback mode.

Step 1: Calibrate (no lens) → d_ref
Step 2: Place lens → camera refocuses → d_lens
Power = 1/d_lens − 1/d_ref
Tips for accuracy
  • Point at a distant, high-contrast object (wall edge, window frame, text on a far board)
  • Keep the camera completely still during each cycle
  • Hold lens perpendicular to camera axis, centred over lens
  • Use good, even lighting — no direct glare on lens
  • Keep lens at consistent distance during each measurement
  • More cycles = better accuracy — aim for all 10
